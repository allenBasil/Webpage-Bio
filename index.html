About Me

I'm a computer science student at Texas A&M University with hands-on experience building full-stack applications, machine learning pipelines, and data-driven tools. I've worked on internships and research projects where I design user interfaces, implement server-side APIs, and ship production-ready software.

My background includes modern web development with React, Flask, and Node.js; data and ML work with Python, PyTorch, Pandas, and OpenCV; and solid fundamentals in C++, Java, SQL, and Git. I also have experience cleaning and visualizing data, building ETL pipelines, and deploying tools that non-technical users rely on every day.

Accessibility Interests

Building software that people actually rely on has taught me that accessibility is not an afterthought — it is a core engineering requirement. During my internship I shipped a browser-based compliance tool used daily by non-technical reviewers, which made me acutely aware of how design choices affect real users. That experience pushed me to learn the WCAG and apply its principles in every interface I build.

Topics I Am Exploring

  • Semantic HTML and its impact on screen readers and assistive technology
  • Color contrast, typography, and readability for users with low vision
  • Keyboard navigation and visible focus management across complex forms
  • Writing meaningful alternative text for data visualizations and images
  • Accessible single-page application patterns in React and TypeScript

The W3C Web Accessibility Initiative introduction is a resource I return to often because it frames accessibility not just as compliance, but as a design philosophy that benefits everyone.
